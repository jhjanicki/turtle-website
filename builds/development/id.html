Sea Turtle Identification

Green Sea Turtle

Carapace

4 lateral scutes & 5 central scutes

Sunburst / radiating pattern on scutes on carapace

The lateral scutes of the carapace are nearly the same size as the central scutes.

Face

One pair prefrontal scales (scales between eyes)

Small head

Round eyes

Other

Mouth: Greens sea turtles have a beak with finely serrated edges, like the teeth of a saw, which enable them to tear seagrasses and scrape algae off of hard surfaces.

Flipper: One claw on front flipper

Diet

Adults are referred to as herbivores although as hatchlings they are omnivores. Their diet consists primarily of algae, seagrasses, and seaweed. Greens have a finely serrated (sawlike) beak that allows them to scrape algae off rocks and tear grasses and seaweeds.

Loggerhead Sea Turtle

Carapace

5 lateral scutes
5 central scutes
Reddish brown carapace

Face

More than one pair prefrontal scales
Big, fat head

Other

Mouth: Loggerheads have strong, massive jaws which enable them to crush hard-shelled prey like conchs and whelks. Leatherbacks have sharp, pointed cusps on their jaws which enable them to pierce and hold onto jellyfish.

Flippers: Two claws on front flipper

Diet

Adults are carnivores, eating crabs, conchs, whelks, and horseshoe crabs. Hatchlings are omnivores, eating both plant and animal material. Loggerheads have a massive head and strong jaws which enable them to crush hard-shelled prey.

Hawksbill Sea Turtle

The central scutes are imbricated (overlapped) and the beak is "hooked"

Carapace

4 lateral scutes
5 central scutes
Central scutes on carapace overlapping
The rear edge of the carapace is deeply serrated.
Thick carapace scutes, with streaks of brown and black on an amber background

Face

Two pairs of prefrontal scales
Small, narrow head that tapers to a distinct hooked beak

Other

Diet

Often referred to as spongivores because of their specialized diet which consists almost exclusively of sponges. The hawksbill has a sharp, narrow "bird-like" beak that allows them to reach within crevices on the reef.

Olive Ridley Turtle

Smallest of the sea turtles

Carapace

6 or more lateral scutes
6 or more central scutes
Dark gray carapace, nearly round

Face

More than one pair prefrontal scales

Other

Two claws on each flipper

Diet

An omnivore, these turtles eat a variety of animals and plants including crabs, shrimp, lobster, urchins, jellies, algae, and fish.
